Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really send unlimited emails from unlimited accounts?

Yes! You can send unlimited emails from unlimited accounts. If you warm-up your accounts properly you can send up to 250-300 per day per account.

The only limits are the number of accounts that you have and number of browser profiles that your computer can handle at the same time.

What's the difference between sending from Gmail SMTP server and sending from the web interface?

According to google the sending limits when using the Gmail web interface directly is 500 per day VS 100-150 a day when using their SMTP servers.

But the practical realistic numbers are: 50-70 with their SMTP servers and 250-300 with the web interface.

Will Gmail delete or block my accounts?

It totally depends on how you use the software, your accounts quality and your emails content.

  • Warm-up your accounts properly.
  • Don’t start sending 50 emails per day from day 1. start slowly and increase your numbers each day.
  • Use spintax and custom fields customization to make your emails looks unique.
  • Don’t use more than 5 accounts per proxy.
  • Always clean your lists.
  • Set delays between emails and respect the Gmail daily limits.

Will my emails land in the recipients spam folder?

  • The content of your emails is too salesy or contains spammy words that trigger spam filters. Avoid spam trigger words.
  • Avoid using links in the first email.
  • Tracking openers and clickers can often trigger spam filters. Avoid tracking pixels if it’s possible at least on new accounts.
  • Your proxy or IP address is flagged by Gmail.
  • Decrease your daily emails and increase delays between emails.
  • Your HTML triggers spam filter: Change your content or use plain text if it’s possible.
